Solutions for Open Land Administration (SOLA)

In December 2012, the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO) successfully concluded the Solutions for Open Land Administration (SOLA) project. The 2.5 year project was funded by the Government of Finland and dedicated to implementing an open source computerised cadastre and registration system that is both affordable and sustainable in the context of developing countries. The open source software produced by the SOLA project (also called SOLA) is based on international good practice for service delivery, responsible governance of tenure (including transparency of process and transparency of tenure details), robust data management and the need for enterprise scale software. The underlying data structures used by the SOLA software are also based on the Land Administration Domain Model (LADM), currently published as the ISO 19152 International Standard by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO).

As the laws and practices governing land administration are often unique to a given country, SOLA was designed to be customisable so that it could appropriately reflect the laws and practices of the host country. To test the effectiveness of the software and the approach used for customisation, the SOLA project included three pilot implementations in Ghana, Nepal and Samoa. Each pilot implementation was undertaken by a team of local software developers who were first trained by an FAO SOLA Mentor so they could undertake the required customizations, support the pilot implementations and maintain the software. Each pilot presented its own challenges with many lessons learned by the FAO SOLA Team along the way. Although customisation of the software was completed for all three pilots, Samoa is the only country that has so far deployed SOLA in support of their day to day land administration activities with Nepal expected to follow suit in the near future.

Customization of the initial generic SOLA software is needed to cater for the laws, existing records and practices of each country where SOLA is implemented as well as to accommodate the specific responsibilities of the land administration agency that will use SOLA in support of their cadastre and registration functions. To assist the customization process, local software developers have been recruited and trained so they can undertake the required customizations, support the pilot implementations and maintain the software. The local software development teams are expected to become key members of the SOLA user and developer community.

Continued Support for SOLA

Since the completion of the SOLA project, interest in SOLA has continued to grow with several new countries now undertaking SOLA customisations. Those countries include Tonga, Lesotho and two implementations (for two states) in Nigeria. These new implementations have been made possible with the help of development assistance donors including the South Pacific Subregional Office of FAO, the Millennium Challenge Account – Lesotho (MCA-L), Adam Smith International and the Department for International Development (DFID) UK.

FAO also continues to support SOLA through the assistance available to countries wanting to implement the Voluntary Guidelines on Responsible Governance of Tenure. In this new context, SOLA is seen as an excellent example of an enabling technology that improves transparency and service delivery in land administration agencies – key elements to improve land tenure governance. Several new modules have also been proposed that are complementary to the cadastral and land registration functions already supported by SOLA. Those modules include:

  • Lease Management
  • State Land Management
  • Mass Property Appraisal/Valuation
  • Cadastral Quality Assurance (Plan Examination)
  • An interface and repository for community recording of land rights (crowd sourcing)

Development of these new modules will be dependent on the requirements of new countries undertaking SOLA implementations, however new funding is also being sought to accelerate the development of the modules within the generic SOLA product.
